Shah Rukh Khan

Indian Actor

Here's a time line of SRK's life

Early life

    Shah Rukh Khan, born on 2 November 1965 in New Delhi, comes from a Muslim family with diverse roots. He spent his early years in Mangalore and later grew up in Rajendra Nagar, New Delhi. Excelling in studies and sports at St. Columba's School, he initially aspired to pursue a sports career but turned to acting due to a shoulder injury. Khan's talent for acting was recognized during his youth, and he studied acting under Barry John at Delhi's Theatre Action Group. Afterward, he briefly attended Hansraj College and Jamia Millia Islamia before focusing on his acting career. Tragically, he lost his father to cancer in 1981 and his mother to diabetes in 1991. Taking on the responsibility of caring for his older sister, Shahnaz, who still lives with him in his Mumbai mansion.

Films

    Shah Rukh Khan, a Bollywood icon, began with TV series "Fauji" and "Circus" before his successful film debut in "Deewana" (1992). He excelled in romantic roles in the '90s with hits like "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge" and "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai." In the 2000s, he explored diverse roles in films like "Swades" and "Chak De! India."
    Khan faced ups and downs but regained fame with "Om Shanti Om" and "Chennai Express." His performance in "My Name Is Khan" received critical acclaim. After a mix of successes and underperformers, "Pathaan" (2023) marked his return to stardom.
    He remains a Bollywood icon with a massive global fan following.

Awards

    Shah Rukh Khan, a renowned Bollywood actor, has won 14 Filmfare Awards, including eight for Best Actor. He received the Padma Shri from the Government of India in 2005 and honors from the Government of France.
    Although he hasn't won a National Film Award, he holds the record for most Best Actor awards tied with Dilip Kumar.
    Khan has also been bestowed with five honorary doctorates from different universities.

IPL team

    Shah Rukh Khan, along with Juhi Chawla and Jay Mehta, bought the Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) team in the IPL for US$75.09 million in 2008.
    Initially struggling, KKR's performance improved, winning the IPL championship in 2012 and 2014. Khan also performed at IPL's opening ceremonies.
    In 2012, he faced a ban from Wankhede Stadium for an altercation with security staff after a match, but the ban was lifted in 2015. The Mumbai Police later cleared him of accusations of being drunk or abusive during the incident in 2012.
